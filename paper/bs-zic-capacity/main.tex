\begin{abstract}
We give the capacity region, in closed form, of the two-user binary symmetric
Z-interference channel $Y_1=X_1\oplus X_2\oplus N_1$, $Y_2=X_2\oplus N_2$ with
independent noises $N_i\sim\mathrm{Bern}(p_i)$, $p_1,p_2\in(0,\tfrac12)$, for
all parameters. For $p_1\le p_2$ the region is
$\{R_1{+}R_2\le 1-h(p_1),\ R_2\le 1-h(p_2)\}$, an instantiation of classical
strong-interference results. For $p_1>p_2$ (weak interference) the region
equals that of the degraded binary symmetric broadcast channel with parameters
$(p_1,p_2)$: $R_2\le 1-h(p_2)$ and
$R_1\le 1-h\bigl(q\st h^{-1}(h(p_2)+R_2)\bigr)$ with $q=(p_1-p_2)/(1-2p_2)$;
treating interference as noise with an optimized input bias is
capacity-achieving and rate splitting is unnecessary. The weak-regime converse
is an eight-line application of Mrs.\ Gerber's Lemma. The result evaluates, in
closed form, the single-letter characterization implied by Liu--Goldsmith for
this channel class.
\end{abstract}

\section{Model and result}

Two senders, two receivers: $Y_1=X_1\oplus X_2\oplus N_1$,
$Y_2=X_2\oplus N_2$, with $N_1\sim\mathrm{Bern}(p_1)$,
$N_2\sim\mathrm{Bern}(p_2)$ independent of each other and of the inputs,
$p_1,p_2\in(0,\tfrac12)$; receiver $i$ decodes message $i$ only, and the
encoders are independent. $h$ is the binary entropy,
$h^{-1}:[0,1]\to[0,\tfrac12]$ its inverse, $a\st b=a(1-b)+b(1-a)$.

\begin{theorem}\label{thm}
The capacity region of the BS-ZIC is
\begin{itemize}
\item[(i)] if $p_1\le p_2$:\quad
$\{(R_1,R_2)\ge 0:\ R_1+R_2\le 1-h(p_1),\ R_2\le 1-h(p_2)\}$;
\item[(ii)] if $p_1>p_2$, with $q=\frac{p_1-p_2}{1-2p_2}\in(0,\tfrac12)$:
\[
\{(R_1,R_2)\ge 0:\ R_2\le 1-h(p_2),\ \
R_1\le 1-h\bigl(q\st h^{-1}(h(p_2)+R_2)\bigr)\}.
\]
The weak-regime sum capacity is $1-h(p_2)$, attained only at
$(0,\,1-h(p_2))$.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof of (i): strong-to-moderate interference}

\emph{Converse.} Since $p_1\le p_2$, write $p_2=p_1\st p_0$ and couple
$N_2^n=N_1^n\oplus N_0^n$ with $N_0\sim\mathrm{Bern}(p_0)$ i.i.d.\
independent; this preserves the law of $(X_2^n,Y_2^n)$, so data processing
gives $I(X_2^n;Y_2^n)\le I(X_2^n;X_2^n\oplus N_1^n)
= I(X_2^n;Y_1^n\mid X_1^n)$ (given $X_1^n$, $Y_1^n$ is a bijection of
$X_2^n\oplus N_1^n$; encoders independent). With Fano,
$n(R_1{+}R_2)\le I(X_1^n,X_2^n;Y_1^n)+n\epsilon_n
\le n(1-h(p_1))+n\epsilon_n$, and $nR_2\le n(1-h(p_2))+n\epsilon_n$.

\emph{Achievability.} Uniform inputs; receiver 1 jointly decodes both
messages over the mod-2 MAC ($R_1,R_2,R_1{+}R_2\le 1-h(p_1)$); receiver 2
needs $R_2\le 1-h(p_2)$, which implies the MAC's $R_2$ constraint when
$p_1\le p_2$. Regions match~\cite{Sato81,CEG87,EGK11}.\qed

\section{Proof of (ii): weak interference}

\emph{Converse.} Now $p_1=p_2\st q$; couple $N_1^n=N_2^n\oplus Q^n$,
$Q\sim\mathrm{Bern}(q)$ i.i.d.\ independent, preserving the law of
$(X_1^n,X_2^n,Y_1^n)$. Let $V^n=X_2^n\oplus N_2^n$ (distributed as $Y_2^n$).
Fano at receiver 2: $\tfrac1nH(V^n)\ge h(p_2)+R_2-\epsilon_n$. Mrs.\ Gerber's
Lemma \cite{WZ73}: $\tfrac1nH(V^n\oplus Q^n)\ge
h\bigl(q\st h^{-1}(\tfrac1nH(V^n))\bigr)$. Hence
\[
nR_1\le n-H(Y_1^n\mid X_1^n)+n\epsilon_n=n-H(V^n\oplus Q^n)+n\epsilon_n
\le n\bigl[1-h\bigl(q\st h^{-1}(h(p_2)+R_2-\epsilon_n)\bigr)\bigr]
+n\epsilon_n,
\]
using $H(Y_1^n\mid X_1^n)=H(X_2^n\oplus N_1^n)=H(V^n\oplus Q^n)$ and
monotonicity of $h(q\st h^{-1}(\cdot))$. Let $n\to\infty$.

\emph{Achievability.} Treat interference as noise with a biased input:
$X_2\sim\mathrm{Bern}(\pi)$ i.i.d., $X_1\sim\mathrm{Bern}(\tfrac12)$.
Receiver 2: $R_2=h(\pi\st p_2)-h(p_2)$. Receiver 1: $Y_1=X_1\oplus Z$ with
$Z\sim\mathrm{Bern}(\pi\st p_1)$, so $R_1=1-h(\pi\st p_1)$. With
$s=\pi\st p_2$ we have $h(s)=h(p_2)+R_2$ and, since $p_1=p_2\st q$,
$\pi\st p_1=s\st q$; hence $R_1=1-h\bigl(q\st h^{-1}(h(p_2)+R_2)\bigr)$ ---
the inner bound traces the converse curve exactly as $\pi$ runs over
$[0,\tfrac12]$. Lower points follow by time sharing.\qed

\section{Discussion}

In the weak regime the BS-ZIC region coincides with the degraded BSC
broadcast region \cite{WZ73}: receiver 1 sees user 2's codeword exactly as a
degraded broadcast ``cloud,'' and Mrs.\ Gerber's Lemma converts the
degradation into a converse, as in Wyner--Ziv. No rate splitting is needed
anywhere in the parameter space, and weak-regime sum-optimality forces user 1
silent. The channel satisfies the class conditions of Liu--Goldsmith
\cite{LG09} exactly and in $n$-letter form (mod-2 additivity gives an
invariant conditional output entropy, and the uniform input maximizes the
interfered receiver's output entropy irrespective of the interference law),
so part (ii) can be read as a closed-form evaluation of their
characterization with a degenerate auxiliary. Moreover, after relabeling
users and writing $N_1 \overset{d}{=} N_2 \oplus \mathrm{Bern}(q)$, the weak
regime falls into Benzel's class of discrete additive degraded interference
channels \cite{Benzel79} (see also \cite{LU06}), so part (ii) should be
regarded as classical; the contribution of this note is the assembled
closed-form statement for all parameters and the short self-contained
Mrs.\ Gerber converse.
